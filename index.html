Day-To-Day Work

Arrive at the athletic training facility to organize and prep for practices and games that day. Tape athletes for practice. Guide student-athletes through rehabilitation exercises. Evaluate and treat injuries that occur during practice or games and provide feedback .

×

Biology Concepts

Topics like biology, anatomy, nutrition, sports medicine, and kinesiology are heavily incorporated into work.

×

Required Education

Obtain a bachelor's degree (common majors are athletic training and exercise science.) Graduate from a Commission on Accreditation of Athletic Trainer Education (CAATE).

×

Salary Range and Work Setting

Athletic trainers typically earn between $40,000 and $80,000 a year. Top professionals can make over $90,000. They work in schools, colleges, clinics, and with sports teams. Some work in the military or corporate settings. Hours can include evenings, weekends, and travel.

×

Why this work?

I want to become an athletic trainer because I enjoy helping others stay healthy, love sports, and I'm interested in how the human body moves and heals. It's a rewarding, hands-on healthcare career which will give me useful knowledge about my bodies for the rest of your lifetime.
